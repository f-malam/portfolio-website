INK TANKResponsive Tattoo Application

| UX Project |

January 2018 - June 2018

Ink Tank is a responsive web app that provides information and inspiration from wide range of Tattoos designs and artists, specializing in different styles. As you can imagine, choosing a permanent tattoo design is an incredible personal experience but it can be quite stressful and complicated. Therefore, Ink Tank app offers a step by step guide and services for Tattoo enthusiast and Tattoo seekers to help with their Tattoo Planning. This is the story of how I  designed the app that aims to deliver a pleasant experience during the Tattoo planning and make people feel confident with their Tattoo decision at the end, with no regrets.

To comply with my confidentiality agreement I have omitted and appropriated confidential information.

Project

My Role

UX Design

As part of my UX design course at Career Foundry, I was given a project brief to build a Tattoo Application from scratch. During this intensive course, all elements in the UX design process are extensively discussed and implemented. Following the project brief, I was responsible for the strategy and design of the responsive web application. I led the the UX project involving competitive Research, User strategy, Testing & interface design, following Design Thinking process.

The challenge was to create a product from scratch using softwares which I was not familiar with. To overcome this challenge, I learnt and practice the softwares and focused on 3 features of the project. There was also a time restraint on the project. This was a 6 month intensive course project but I managed to finish the project 1 month early using my Time mamangement skills.

Research & Analysis

Competitive Research

The approach to Understanding

First, I conducted an extensive research on the Tattoo market and existing Tattoo apps in order to  understand the industry and potential users.  I found two leading companies in the tattoo app industry that offers similar services. One is Tattoodo and another one is InkHunter. Both apps are really good and fun, however, after completing a full competitive analysis including; competitors profile, market profile, business strategy, SWOT analysis & UX/UI analysis , I found their services limited and lacked a lot of information and guidance.  In having said that, I was able to find and determine the problem that users are currently experiencing.

Problem & Objective

The Challenge

The existing Tattoo apps do not seem to address the complexity and emotional element of finding and choosing the right Tattoo design and the right Tattoo Artist. Many of these apps tend to lack reliable information and guidance, which results into many people regretting their decision at the end. In order to make users enjoy their Tattoo experience and prevent any regrets, I planned to design and develop a platform that offers a personalized service to help with the whole Tattoo planning.​​​​​​​

Interview

Focusing on Goals

Following an extensive research with competitors, it was important for me to understand and identify with potential users. Therefore, I engaged with my target audience by interviewing and observing them on behavior and attitude. The main research goals were; 

  1. Understand how Tattoo seekers find, choose and decide on a Tattoo designs and Tattoo Artists.
  2. Understand any concerns and issues that may arise in the process of looking or getting a Tattoo.
  3. Determine what features and functions meet users needs and demands.

By focusing on potential users who either have a Tattoo, who is looking to get a new Tattoo, and who is interesting in getting a Tattoo for the first time, it helped me gain more in-depth insights.

Maps

Affinity Maps

Sorting & Mapping

I created an affinity map using the insights collected during the Interview. From the insights, I identified behaviors, goals, and attitudes of the participants towards a similar Tattoo apps and services that they have encountered during their Tattoo process. This helped me understand users problems and needs more clearly. ​​​​​​​

One statement found from all users at one point in the interview was, and quote, 

'I don't want to have any regrets'

I found this statement very moving and effective because it made me understand that this is one of the biggest challenges users face everyday when going through a Tattoo process. This made me put myself in their position and helped me generate ideas to find solution to their problems and needs. 

User Personas

Point Of View

Building my Audience

​​​​​​​​​​​​From the research and user interviews, I was able to define 3 types of users in different stages of a Tattoo journey; The beginner, The Tattoo Lover and The Tattoo enthusiast. This led me to create my persona's in order to help me focus on the user’s problems and priorities on relevant functions and features in my design to meet their needs, which in turn help speed up the process.

Usability Test

Usability Test

Working with Humans

Wireframes and prototypes were tested constantly and iterated progressively to determine the user flows and put these to test with potential users from my target audience. I created working prototypes for mobile and desktop versions in Invision to conduct interactive tests with live and virtual test users.

Usability test was conducted on both in-person and remote with potential users, to define the task, establish objectives & evaluate the app. In order to ensure the reliability of the test, usability test focused on 3 core features of the Ink Tank app and they are as follows;

  1. Tattoo Guide
  2. Tattoo Design 
  3. Tattoo Artists

Results

Usability Test Results

Analyzing Results & Making Improvements

Test revealed participants found it easy to navigate through the Ink tank app. However, usability issues were identified that was related to information, layout and visibility. Iterations were made to solve these issues. I was able to find appropriate solutions to  improve  the app for better user experience, whilst focusing on users needs at all times. This was re-tested for validation.

View problems and solutions

One of the issues particpants found was the lack of information about the Artists. This was solved by adding a 'About' section with detail informaiton of Artist's current status, history, their story and inspiration etc, on the Artist detail page.

Another issue participants encountered was the lack of knowledge about the app before login. Few users were unaware that they could access more information about the app before getting into the app. This was resolved by adding 'Swipe for more' text on the welcome page.

For further testing, I decided to conduct a preference (A/B) test on the welcome page. The reason I chose to test the welcome page is because it is the first impression users have about the app. Therefore it was important to test out what users prefer and what meets their needs before even getting into the app. 

Preference (A/B) Test: Welcome Page

The results indicated 60% of users preferred option 1: with a ‘swipe for more’ text. The reasons behind their choice are based on the fact that it was a clearer guidance to swipe for more information about the app. 40% preferred the option 2: without ‘swipe for more’ text. They found the dots to be self-intuitive. Overall, option 1 welcome page would be most effective for better user experience of Ink Tank App. Based on the results, I updated the welcome page.

It was very useful to get feedback from potential users based on their reaction to the app prototype. The results allowed me to make necessary changes and continue to make improvements.
