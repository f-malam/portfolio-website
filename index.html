About Me

“To live a creative life, we must lose our fear of being wrong.”
— Joseph Chilton Pearce'

HELLO! I am a UX Designer based in London, UK.

I come primarily from an extensive psychology background, with bachelor degree in Psychology and Masters in psychology research from Kings College London. I have always been fascinated of how brain works and how we perceive and retain information. On that note, combining my passion for technology with psychology, I decided to further develop my skills in Human computer interaction, as a UX designer. I enjoy turning complex problems into simple, beautiful and intuitive designs.

My professional journey continued as I nurtured my creative side through web/mobile-design and photography, which extended my ability to develop and design a more effective solutions, allowing me to focus my energy on the task at hand. When I’m in my element, I’m creating, but I understand that creativity without context lacks purpose.

As a user experience (UX) designer and a Psychologist, I have a deep interest in how people connect through verbal and visual communication, with great capacity for emotional and cognitive empathy. I attempt to integrate my knowledge of psychology into everything I design.

When I’m not thinking about (or actually doing) design, I love travelling, taking pictures on my new camera, horse riding, spending time with friends, belly-dancing or trying out new restaurants and cafes.

My Design Process

Research

The first step for any project that I take is all about doing my due diligence. I like to get the feel of what kind of issues and problems users have already experienced and look at what solutions to similar problems have already been proposed.

I also like to get qualitative information directly from users by simply having a conversation and observing them, following a script of questions and recording the results. From this I find to get honest, direct feedback from current or potential users. Helps me understand and gain more insight.

  1. Competitive Analysis
  2. Interveiws
  3. Surveys

Define

From extensive research, I create my personas. User personas help me better understand user demographics, needs, goals, emotions, behaviors, fears, and further limitations.

Once I identify users’ goals, its fun mapping out possible tasks to see how it would it look like.

  1. Information Architecture
  2. User personas
  3. User Flows
  4. Affinity Maps
  5. Card Sorting

Ideate

Using pencil and paper, I love to sketch out ideas and start developing blueprints of the design. By building a practical, preliminary version of the product, I am able to catch potential problems and promising insights faster and earlier in the process.

The good thing about prototyping in this way is that it is a great way to find the middle ground between overspending and overthinking, between too little investment and too much user validation.

  1. Brainstorming
  2. Sketches
  3. StoryBoards

Prototypes

Creating wireframe & prototypes helps me learn and see how users would interact with the product. I focus on specific features and functions of the product, depending on the maturity of the project.

  1. Wireframes
  2. Non-Interactive Prototypes
  3. Interactive Prototypes

Test & Analytics

This is the fun part. I love to test out the prototypes so I can directly observe potential users behavior and interaction with my prototype. On that note, I love recieving feedback so I can make further iterations in order to meet users needs and goals.

  1. User Testing
  2. User Analysis
  3. Iteration

UI

Using Gestalt Psychology and visual design principles to my prototypes, I focus on the aesthetics of the product and how those aesthetics enhance the user experience. To that end, I incorporate aspects of visual perception in organizing visual information.

Designing with these principles in mind, helps me create a more seamless experience between UX and UI, improving the overall usability of the product.

  1. Color & Typography
  2. UI kit
  3. Styles

Tools I Use

Low/Mid Fidelity

I love to use pen and paper to explore different variations of ideas in a short amount of time. I also use wireframing tools like Balsamic to add more details in order to create a finalized mockup.

High Fidelity

Projects that require high-fidelity prototypes with significant visual emphasis, I tend to use Sketch as my main tool. I sometime use Photoshop and Illustrator.

Interactive Prototype

When it comes to interactive prototyping and testing, I like to use tools like Invision and Prott.
